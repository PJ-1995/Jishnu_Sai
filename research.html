My doctoral research is dedicated to investigating the non-Gaussian features of primordial fluctuations. Specifically, I utilize various non-Gaussian correlators to explore the correlation between inflationary perturbations and primordial magnetic fields during the inflationary epoch. This work aims to constrain and distinguish between different models of inflation and inflationary magnetogenesis.

However, I am interested in broad range of topics in theoretical cosmology: Inflation and alternatives, primordial non-Gaussianity, cosmological correlators, cosmological bootstrap, cosmological collider, primordial magnetic fields, cosmic microwave background, cosmological soft theorems, quantum field theory in curved spacetime, cosmological backreaction, $\delta N$ formalism, primordial blackholes, IR effects in de Sitter, quantum loop effects in the early universe.
